4 Job Workspace — Complete Calibration Workflow

Click Open on any job to enter the Job Workspace. This is the most feature-rich screen in CLMS. It contains five tabs:

4.1 Datasheet Tab

This is where the actual calibration measurements are entered. The datasheet records Before and After calibration readings for each measurement point.

4.1.1 Accuracy Class Selector

At the top of the Datasheet tab, select the instrument's accuracy class. This determines:

  • MPE (Maximum Permissible Error) — The tolerance threshold for PASS/FAIL evaluation
  • Minimum Readings required per point (per NABL 129 criteria)
  • Calibration Interval recommendation in months

Record the laboratory environmental conditions at the time of calibration. NABL requires these to be within controlled limits.

⚠️ If temperature or humidity is outside the NABL-specified range, the input field turns red and a warning alert appears. Do not proceed with calibration until conditions are within spec or document a deviation justification.

4.1.3 Measurement Readings

Enter the Before and After calibration readings for each nominal value point. The system automatically calculates:

  • Mean of multiple readings per point
  • Error (difference from nominal)

4.2 Uncertainty Tab

The Uncertainty tab implements a GUM-compliant uncertainty budget calculation. This is mandatory for NABL accredited laboratories.

The system lists all Uncertainty Sources (Type A and Type B) relevant to this instrument type.
For each source, enter or confirm the value, distribution type (Normal/Rectangular/U-shaped), and divisor.
The system calculates Standard Uncertainty (u) for each source.
Combined Uncertainty (Uc) is computed as the square root of the sum of squares.
Expanded Uncertainty (U) = Uc × Coverage Factor k (usually k=2 for 95% confidence).

5.3 Calibration Due Tracking

After each calibration, the system automatically sets lastCalibrationDate and calculates nextDueDate based on the calibration interval. The instrument list shows a colour-coded status:

  • OK — Due date more than 30 days away
  • Due Soon — Due within 30 days
  • Overdue — Due date has passed

7.1 Adding a Reference Standard

Click Add Standard.
Enter: Standard Name, ID Number, Make, Model, Serial Number, Traceability, Certificate Number, Uncertainty, Calibrated Date, Calibration Due Date, Location.

7.2 Alerts

  • Red banner: One or more standards are OVERDUE — do not use for calibration
  • Orange banner: Standards due within 30 days — schedule recalibration
🚨 Using an OVERDUE reference standard for calibration is a major non-conformance under NABL criteria and could result in suspension of your accreditation. Always check the Reference Standards page before starting calibration work.
